Druidic Circle of the City

Within every city is the same air that lows through forests and above seas. All manner of lora and fauna call the streets, sewers, and rooftops their home; rats tunnel through walls, creeping moss blankets brick, birds lay nests, and bugs infest every corner. Pockets of nature endure nestled within civilization, and where nature goes, druids follow. Druids of the Circle of the City embody nature's quietp ersistence, preferring a subtle hand and quick eye in defense of their small, hidden sanctuaries.

Urban Jungles

At 2nd level, you effortlessly blend with the critters that make towns and cities their homes. You can transform into Tiny beasts with a challenge rating of 0 without expending a use of your Wild Shape feature. Your druid level deturmines the beasts you can transform into, as shown in the beast shapes table.

Circle Spells

Starting at 2nd level, your communion with the wild places and creatures within civilization allows you to cast certain spells, as shown on the Circle Spells table. Once you gain access to a circle spell, you always have it prepared, and it doesn't count against the number of spells you can prepare each day. If you gain access to a spell that doesn't appear on the druid spell list, the spell is nonetheless a druid spell for you.

Gutter Runner

At 6th level, life in the city means toughing it, and knowing when to quit. When you take damage, you can use your reaction to reduce the damage by half. Additionally, after you take the damage, you can use your Wild Shape feature (no action required) and move up to half your speed without provoking opportunity attacks. You can use this feature twice. You regain all expended uses of it when you finish a short or long rest.

Commune with Civilization

You always have it prepared, and it doesn't count against the number of spells you can prepare each day. Additionally, when you cast commune with nature, the spell functions within towns and cities, giving you knowledge of the area

  • roads, shortcuts, and directions
  • landmarks and frequently visited locations
  • prevalent cultures, beliefs, and savigs

For example, you could determine the best routes to a givven destination ( such as a perfect pathway through the sewers), the location of specific places of buisness, and the prevailing local religion.

Urban Legend

At 14th level, you move through the city like a phantom, appearing anywhere at any time. you can move through earth, stome, wood, or any time. You don't disturb the material you move through, and you take 2d6 dammage if you end your turn within it.
